About Me

I am a Ph.D. candidate in Mechanical Engineering at the University of California, Berkeley. My advisor is Dr. Andy Packard and I work in the Berkeley Center for Control and Identification (BCCI). I am conducting research in the fields of robust and nonlinear controls and uncertainty quantification in high dimensional systems. My minors are in Mathematics and Optimization.


Previously, I received a B.S. from Kansas State University and a M.S. from Colorado State University, both in Mechanical Engineering. After completing my M.S. I worked for two years at OptimumG, a vehicle dynamics consulting and software development company.

Technical Interests

  • Stability and performance certification of large dynamical systems
  • Dissipative dynamical system theory
  • Nonlinear and robust system analysis and control
  • Sum of Squares Programming
  • Optimization theory and algorithms
